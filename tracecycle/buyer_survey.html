TraceCycle Buyer Assessment Survey

Instructions: Please answer all questions. When you're done, click "Generate CSV Download" to download your responses. Then email the CSV file to us.

Section 1: Side-Selling & Volume Loss

Q1. How many farmers sell to you?
Q2. Last year, you gave farmers training or loans or inputs. How many of these farmers stopped selling to you or now sell much less to you?
Q3. Farmers say they sold 1000 kg to you. When it arrives at your factory, you receive less than 1000 kg. How much do you usually lose?
Q4. Can you tell which farmers are selling to your competitors?
Q5. A farmer stops selling to you after you gave them a loan or training. When do you usually learn about this?
Q6. You spend money on farmer loans, training, and inputs each year. How much of this money is wasted on farmers who stop selling to you or sell to competitors?
Q7. How often do you argue with farmers or cooperatives because the amount they say they sold does not match the amount you received?

Section 2: Training and Loan Returns

Q8. After you train farmers, how do you check if each farmer is doing what you taught them?
Q9. When you decide which farmers get loans, what information do you look at?
Q10. Can you make a list showing which farmers got better after your training and which farmers did not get better?
Q11. You trained 100 farmers. How many of these 100 farmers actually do what you taught them?
Q12. How do you track which farmers owe you money and if they are paying back their loans?
Q13. A farmer does not pay back their loan. When do you find out?

Section 3: Proving Where Product Comes From

Q14. In the past year, how many times did buyers ask you to prove which farms your product came from?
Q15. When buyers ask you to prove which farms your product came from, what can you give them?
Q16. In the past year, did you lose sales because you could not prove which farms your product came from?
Q17. You sell certified product (like Fairtrade, RSPO, Rainforest Alliance, Organic). Can you tell buyers which exact farms this certified product came from?
Q18. Buyers pay you extra money for certified product. How do you make sure this extra money goes to the correct farmers who grew that certified product?
Q19. If a buyer asks "Show me GPS map of every farm that grew the product in this shipment," how long does it take you to provide this?
Q20. In the past year, did buyers threaten to stop buying from you because you could not prove where your product came from?

Section 4: Planning Factory and Transport

Q21. How many weeks before harvest starts can you correctly predict how much product will come?
Q22. Last year, how accurate was your harvest prediction compared to what actually arrived?
Q23. During harvest, how often does your factory have too much product or too little product because you did not predict correctly?
Q24. Can you tell which region will harvest first, which region will harvest second, etc.?
Q25. Last harvest season, how much product went bad or was wasted because factory could not process it in time?

Section 5: Record Keeping Systems

Q26. What do you use to keep records about farmers and what they sell?
Q27. If I ask "Show me Farmer John's complete history: everything he sold, all quality grades, all training he received, all loans," how long does it take?
Q28. Where do you keep farmer information? (Select the main place)
Q29. When farmers sell at a collection point, how is it recorded?
Q30. How many different places do you write the same information? (Example: farmer name, amount sold, quality)

Section 6: Supplier Performance & Advanced Features

Q31. Can you compare performance across different cooperatives you work with?
Q32. Do you offer advance payments to farmers before harvest in exchange for guaranteed delivery?
Q33. When you offer advance payments before harvest, how many farmers fail to deliver the product they promised?
Q34. Can you identify your top-quality producers and pay them different prices than lower-quality producers?
