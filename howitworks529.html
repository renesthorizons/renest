Understanding Your 529 Benefits

A step-by-step guide to maximizing your graduate student tax benefits

Sara's 529 Journey

Sara, a PhD student in Aerospace Engineering at CU Boulder, used our calculator and discovered she could get a $924 annual tax refund using a 529 plan for her qualified expenses. Here's how Renest helps her get there:

The Student

Sara is a PhD student in Aerospace Engineering at CU Boulder, managing a busy academic schedule while trying to make the most of her $39,000 stipend. Like many graduate students, she spends much of her income on rent, groceries, and other qualified expenses.

I was excited to learn I could get over $900 back for money I was already spending, but initially I was worried about more paperwork.

Within 5 minutes, Sara opened and linked her Renest account. Then Renest automated her 529 set-up, tracking of her qualified expenses and prepararation of her tax documentation.

It was easy.

Honestly I was surprised I could get $900, but I did and it was great because Renest did not cost anything until I actually got the money they said they could get me.

1

Opening Her 529 Account

Sara is a PhD student in Aerospace Engineering at CU Boulder with a $39,000 annual stipend.

  • She needs to open a CollegeInvest 529 account (Colorado's plan)
  • Required to submit SSN, bank details, and beneficiary information
  • Must designate herself as the beneficiary for grad school
  • Select the high yield savings or fixed income option

With Renest

Renest handles the entire account setup. Sara provides basic info once, and we manage all paperwork and account creation.

2

Planning Qualified Expenses

Sara's monthly qualified expenses she must track and maintain records for 3 years:

  • Rent: $1,350/month in Boulder
  • Utilities and phone bill fees: $50/month
  • Groceries: ~$350/month
  • Total monthly qualified spend: ~$1,750

With Renest

Our system automatically verifies her lease and tracks all qualified expenses. No manual logging needed.

3

Contributing to 529

Sara needs to contribute before spending:

  • Must contribute $1,750 monthly to cover expenses
  • Contributions need to be made before payments
  • Colorado offers 4.4% tax benefit on contributions

With Renest

We handle all transfers automatically and can provide the 4.4% tax benefit upfront.

4

Making Qualified Withdrawals

Each expense requires a matching withdrawal:

  • Must withdraw exact amount of each expense
  • Timing must match within same tax year
  • Records needed for each withdrawal-expense pair

With Renest

Our system manages all withdrawals automatically, ensuring perfect timing and documentation.

5

Tax Time Benefits

Sara's annual benefits:

  • Total qualified expenses: $21,000
  • Colorado tax benefit (4.4%): $924

With Renest

Sara chose to split her benefits: $300 for immediate spending, $324 to retirement savings, and $200 for a new iPad. She only pays Renest after receiving benefits and is confident knowing all her records are maintained with Renest.
