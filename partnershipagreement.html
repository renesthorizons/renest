Design Partnership Framework

Background

UCHealth faces the complex challenge of providing comprehensive benefits to a diverse workforce ranging from highly compensated physicians to essential support staff. Furthermore, UCHealth is committed towards uplifting and serving the community beyond immediate patient-physician interactions. This diversity presents a unique opportunity to innovate in the retirement benefits space.

Renest has developed a novel retirement benefit that addresses key gaps:

  1. Supporting employees who struggle to maximize their retirement benefits
  2. Enabling highly compensated employees to enhance their retirement savings
  3. Maximizing savings for individuals in a community who otherwise cannot afford to

This partnership represents an opportunity to align UCHealth's commitment to employee well-being with Renest's innovative approach to retirement benefits.

Shared Vision & Values

  • Employee Financial Health: Supporting healthcare workers across all compensation levels in building secure financial futures
  • Community Impact: Creating scalable solutions that could benefit the broader healthcare community
  • Mission Alignment: Supporting the healthcare workforce who dedicate themselves to community well-being

Background of Partnership

The guiding principle of this partnership is to explore and identify innovative retirement benefit solutions that serve UCHealth's diverse workforce and / or community while validating Renest's approach to expanding retirement savings access.

Partnership Agreement

By agreeing to this exploratory partnership, Renest and UCHealth agree to the following:

  1. Identify person/s of contact
  2. A follow up meeting within 3 months to:
    • Discuss the scope and status of a Renest partnership
    • Identify challenges UCHealth faces internally or in the community that is aligned with a solution Renest can provide
    • Determine legal considerations

Partnership Scope

Below is an outline with more depth surrounding the scope and purpose of an initial partnership agreement which can serve as a template for our follow-up meeting.

Legal Foundation

  • Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement
  • HIPAA compliance protocols
  • Data privacy and security standards

Focus Areas

  • Employee and Community benefit channel
  • Employee retirement benefit enhancement
  • Platform validation and refinement
  • Process optimization
  • Employee engagement strategies

Success Indicators

  • Employee awareness and engagement
  • Platform utilization
  • User feedback and satisfaction
  • Operational efficiency

Implementation

  • Pilot within selected departments
  • Flexible rollout approach
  • Iterative feedback and adjustment
  • Regular stakeholder communication

Key Contacts

UCHealth

  • HR Leadership
  • Benefits Team
  • Selected Department Leaders

Renest

  • Implementation Team
  • Customer Success
  • Technical Support

Next Steps

Purpose

To establish feasibility and scope of information sharing between Renest and UCHealth

Key Discussion Points

  1. Stakeholder Identification
    • Determine appropriate UCHealth points of contact across hospitals
    • Define decision-making hierarchy
    • Identify key influencers and champions
  2. Information Sharing Parameters
    • Define acceptable data sharing boundaries
    • Establish HIPAA compliance framework
    • Outline security requirements
  3. Initial Timeline
    • Set key milestones
    • Define immediate next steps
    • Establish check-in cadence

Meeting Participants

To be determined by UCHealth leadership, suggested attendees:

  • HR/Benefits Leadership
  • Compliance Representative
  • Department Leader from potential pilot area

Partnership Terms

Duration

Initial 6-month exploratory period with potential for extension

Commitments

UCHealth

  • Provide appropriate access and support for pilot implementation
  • Engage relevant stakeholders
  • Share necessary non-PHI data
  • Participate in regular feedback sessions

Renest

  • Deliver core benefit platform
  • Provide implementation support
  • Maintain data security standards
  • Adapt based on learned insights

General Terms

  • Each party bears their own costs during pilot
  • Regular check-ins to assess progress
  • Mutual flexibility in approach
  • Clear communication channels
